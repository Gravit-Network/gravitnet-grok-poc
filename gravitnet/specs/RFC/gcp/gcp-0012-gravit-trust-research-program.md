# Abstract

This proposal defines the Gravit Trust Research Program.

The research program establishes a long-term scientific and engineering agenda for the study, validation and evolution of trust infrastructure within distributed systems.

The program organizes research around the canonical components of the Gravit protocol, including:

• trust computation models
• adversarial resilience
• network dynamics
• epistemic signal evaluation
• governance and incentive structures

The goal of the research program is to create an open collaborative environment where researchers, engineers and institutions can contribute to the development of trustworthy distributed systems.

---

# Motivation

Trust infrastructure is an emerging field that intersects multiple domains including:

• distributed systems
• cryptography
• artificial intelligence
• network science
• economics and game theory
• epistemology and information theory

Existing research often addresses these domains independently.

The Gravit Trust Research Program aims to unify these perspectives within a coherent framework for studying trust networks.

The research program also provides a structured pathway for validating and evolving the Gravit protocol through scientific collaboration.

---

# Research Objectives

The program defines several high-level objectives.

### Formal Trust Models

Develop advanced mathematical models of trust propagation and reputation dynamics.

---

### Adversarial Analysis

Study adversarial strategies affecting trust networks, including Sybil attacks, collusion clusters and coordinated misinformation.

---

### Large-Scale Network Behavior

Investigate how trust systems behave in large-scale distributed environments with millions of nodes.

---

### Signal Credibility Evaluation

Explore mechanisms for evaluating reliability of signals using epistemic frameworks and statistical methods.

---

### Governance and Coordination

Analyze governance models for decentralized trust infrastructure.

---

### Incentive Structures

Study economic models that align incentives with network integrity.
