# Gravit Trust Manifesto

Version: 0.1
Date: March 2026

We are entering the age of autonomous systems.

Agents, models and decentralized networks will soon act on our behalf — negotiate, verify, transact, govern and decide — faster and at scales beyond human oversight.

Yet every autonomous action rests on one fragile foundation: **trust**.

Without verifiable trust, autonomy becomes fragility.
Without computable trust, coordination becomes chaos.
Without transparent trust, power concentrates in opaque intermediaries.

Humanity now faces a choice:

Either trust remains a scarce, subjective, centralized resource — controlled by platforms, governments or gatekeepers —
or trust becomes open, computable, verifiable infrastructure — as fundamental as TCP/IP, HTTP or electricity.

Gravit exists to make the second path possible.

We believe trust infrastructure should be:

**Computable** — derived from mathematics, not declarations
**Verifiable** — cryptographically provable at every step
**Adversarial-resilient** — designed for a world with rational and malicious actors
**Epistemically grounded** — distinguishing knowledge from speculation, evidence from noise
**Economically aligned** — where honest behavior is the rational choice
**Open & evolvable** — governed transparently, improved through science

Gravit is not a product.
It is not a company.
It is not another blockchain or reputation token.

Gravit is an open attempt to define the next layer of internet infrastructure:

a neutral, public, mathematically sound substrate for trust
in a world where humans are no longer the only decision-makers.

We invite researchers, engineers, philosophers, institutions and builders
to join this exploration.

Because the future will either be built on verifiable trust —
or it will be built on unverifiable power.

We choose the former.

Gravit Core Contributors
Rome, March 2026
